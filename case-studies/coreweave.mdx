### Additional caveats

- **Shares outstanding** were derived from market cap, not from XBRL filings
- **Sales-to-capital ratio** was manually anchored (0.17x current vs. 2.0x assumed) — this is the single most sensitive assumption
- **CoreWeave has no public 10-K prior to FY2025** (IPO March 2025) — no multi-year historical trend to calibrate against
- **FCFF is negative in years 1–8** — the valuation is almost entirely terminal-value-driven
- **Employee options** were detected but not valued — final result may be slightly overstated

## What this case study demonstrates

CoreWeave is a stress-test for the valuation plugin. It hits nearly every edge case:

1. **Recent IPO** — only one annual filing available, no multi-year history
2. **Pre-profitability** — negative EBIT makes many standard calculations (interest coverage, ROIC) unusual
3. **Extreme leverage** — $29.8B of debt on $5.1B of revenue, resulting in a D-rated synthetic bond rating
4. **Hyper-growth** — 168% historical growth, with analyst consensus at 134% for the next year
5. **Capital-intensive** — current sales-to-capital of 0.17x requires manual forward-looking adjustment
6. **Missing XBRL fields** — shares outstanding not in filings, requiring derivation from market cap

The plugin handled all of these programmatically, flagged the risks, and produced a valuation that landed at $111/share — 33% above the market price at the time. Whether you agree with the assumptions or not, every number is traceable to a specific SEC filing or data source, and every judgment call is documented.
