## What it does

Estimates the probability that the company will fail (go bankrupt) before the projected cash flows materialize. The DCF value is then adjusted: `Adjusted value = DCF value × (1 − failure probability)`.

## Two methods

**Bond-rating-based:** Uses historical default rates by bond rating from Damodaran's dataset. If the company has a bond rating (or synthetic rating from the cost-of-capital skill), the historical 10-year cumulative default rate for that rating class is used.

**Age-based:** Uses Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) business survival data. Younger companies have higher failure rates. Used when no bond rating is available.
